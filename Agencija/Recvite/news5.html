How to Get a Commercial Driver’s License (CDL) in 2021

  • Home
  • News and Times
  • Endorsement, Tips

So, are you ready to get your commercial driver’s license or CDL for short? Or are you looking to find out more about it? Below we wrote everything you need to know about how to get CDL.

What is a CDL License?

By now you probably know that CDL is short for a commercial driver’s license. This type of license is required to operate large, heavy, tank or placarded vehicles for commercial purposes. Besides CDL, some commercial motor vehicles or CMVs will require you to have additional special endorsements such as hazmat (hazardous materials), tankers, double/triple trailers, etc. If you are interested in any of the endorsements visit our blog to learn how to acquire those. But for now, let’s talk about how to get a CDL.

Minimum requirements to get a CDL.

These requirements are basic and go for all classes of commercial driver’s licenses – A, B and C. Additionally, there are specific requirements for each license class and you will find them linked to our blog posts specified for those classes.

How to get a CLD step by step:

1. At least 21 years of age or at least 18 years of age to drive the interstate
2. Submit your CDL application and pay the application fee
3. You will need ID and Social Security number verification – check your state requirements
4. Proof of state and US residency
5. Complete and submit medical examination report and certificate form
6. Pass a vision test
7. Pass a knowledge exam
8. At this point, you will receive your Commercial Learner’s Permit or CLP
9. After a minimum of 14 days, you can schedule your CDL road skill examination
10. Pass your pre-trip inspection
11. Pass the road skill and driving test. You will need your own vehicle.
12. If you passed, congratulations! Now all you have to do is pay fees for your CDL and there it is, now you’re officially a commercial driver’s licensed driver!

Important note: Are you a vet? Military veterans with experience in driving military buses or trucks, have a possibility to waive the driving test, but also with proof of relevant military experience your state might allow you to waive general CDL test and endorsements tests such as tanker, hazardous material, or passenger endorsement written test. So check with your state!

Now that you know how to get CDL, we can talk about classes A, B, and C.
