Driver Ghosting; Tips To Reduce It

  • Home
  • News and Times
  • CDL Hire, Recruiment, Tips

Is there anything more annoying than a driver ghosting you after you invested time and money? Have you ever had a driver who was supposed to arrive on Monday? With the airplane ticket you got him, and yet he didn’t? Yep, we’ve all been there. To help you reduce driver ghosting as much as possible we sat down and wrote a few tips.

Speed Up Your Response Time

We can’t stress this enough, but the response time is the key element in recruiting. Also one of the ways to reduce the likelihood of driver ghosting you. After the driver fills up your application form don’t let him wait for too long. Then closer they get to joining your team we suggest more engagement. This will reduce the change of driver ghosting you.

It’s a good idea to set up an automatic email or SMS that the driver will get upon completing the application form. It’s also great to check up on your driver on a bi-daily basis prior to his arrival.

Develop Your Own Approach

When it comes to the application process and communication with drivers everyone does it pretty much the same, so try to stand out. This means you should follow up almost daily with the drivers that are in the on board process. Also customize the application form, ask the driver what is his favorite coffee brand to stock it up for him, it’s a few bucks for you but it’s a feeling of a community for him. This will insanely help to decrease driver ghosting you.

Understand Your Lead Source and The Type of Candidate

It’s important to know what type of candidate you are getting. Is your candidate currently employed and ready to switch for 0.05CPM more than he is paid at the moment? Or for a newer year truck? Is he looking for a long term place where he’ll stay for years to come?

Most of the sources will get you candidates that are just looking for more cents per mile, while others will get you candidates that are looking for a more stable relationship.

This all has a big impact when it comes to recruiting your CDL driver and the way you approach it. It will also help you reduce driver ghosting, so you should always have this in mind.
